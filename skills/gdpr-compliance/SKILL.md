# GDPR Compliance

Guide projects toward compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU 2016/679). This skill covers the key obligations, not legal advice -- always involve qualified legal counsel for binding decisions.

## Lawful Bases for Processing

Every processing activity must rely on one of six lawful bases:

1. **Consent** -- Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. Must be as easy to withdraw as to give.
2. **Contract** -- Processing necessary to fulfill a contract with the data subject.
3. **Legal obligation** -- Required by EU or member state law.
4. **Vital interests** -- Protecting someone's life.
5. **Public interest** -- Processing necessary for a task in the public interest.
6. **Legitimate interests** -- Balanced against the data subject's rights (requires a Legitimate Interest Assessment).

Document the chosen lawful basis for each processing activity in your Records of Processing Activities (ROPA).

## Data Subject Rights

Respond to requests within one calendar month:

- **Access** -- Provide a copy of their personal data and processing details.
- **Rectification** -- Correct inaccurate data without undue delay.
- **Erasure ("right to be forgotten")** -- Delete data when consent is withdrawn or data is no longer necessary.
- **Restriction** -- Limit processing while accuracy or lawfulness is contested.
- **Portability** -- Provide data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Object** -- Allow objection to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.

## Consent Management

- Consent must be opt-in, never pre-ticked.
- Record when, how, and what the user consented to.
- Provide a clear mechanism to withdraw consent at any time.
- Re-obtain consent if the purpose of processing changes.

## Data Protection Officer (DPO)

Appoint a DPO if you are a public authority, carry out large-scale systematic monitoring, or process special-category data at scale. The DPO must operate independently and report to the highest management level.

## Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

Conduct a DPIA before processing that is likely to result in high risk -- profiling, large-scale processing of sensitive data, systematic monitoring of public areas. Document risks, mitigation measures, and residual risk.

## Breach Notification

- Notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach.
- Notify affected data subjects without undue delay if the breach poses a high risk to their rights.
- Document every breach, its effects, and the remedial action taken.

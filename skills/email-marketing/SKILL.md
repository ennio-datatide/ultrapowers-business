# Email Marketing

Design and optimize email marketing campaigns that reach the inbox, get opened, and drive action. Cover subject lines, segmentation, drip campaigns, A/B testing, and deliverability.

## Subject Lines

The subject line determines whether your email gets opened:

- Keep it under 50 characters so it displays fully on mobile.
- Be specific about the value inside ("Your weekly analytics summary" not "Newsletter #47").
- Use the recipient's name or company sparingly -- personalization works but overuse feels automated.
- Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation (!!!), and words like "free", "guaranteed", "act now".
- Test emoji use with your audience -- it lifts open rates for some segments and hurts others.
- Write the preview text (preheader) deliberately; do not let it default to "View in browser".

## Segmentation

Send the right message to the right people:

- **Behavioral** -- Segment by actions: signed up but never activated, active users, churned users, feature-specific usage.
- **Demographic** -- Role, company size, industry, geography.
- **Engagement** -- Segment by email engagement: highly engaged, occasionally opens, inactive (has not opened in 90+ days).
- **Lifecycle stage** -- Prospect, trial, paying customer, at-risk, churned.

Smaller, well-targeted segments outperform large blast sends on every metric.

## Drip Campaigns

Automated sequences triggered by user actions:

### Onboarding Sequence
1. **Welcome** (immediate) -- Confirm signup, set expectations, single next step.
2. **Quick win** (day 1-2) -- Guide them to their first success with the product.
3. **Feature education** (day 3-5) -- Introduce a key feature with a use case.
4. **Social proof** (day 7) -- Customer story or testimonial relevant to their segment.
5. **Check-in** (day 14) -- Ask if they need help, offer a call or resource.

### Re-engagement Sequence
- Target users inactive for 30-90 days.
- Remind them of value, share what is new, offer assistance.
- If no response after 2-3 emails, suppress from regular sends to protect deliverability.

## A/B Testing

- Test one variable at a time: subject line, send time, CTA text, or email length.
- Use a statistically significant sample (at least 1,000 per variant for reliable results).
- Run the test for at least 24 hours to account for timezone differences.
- Measure the metric that matters for your goal: open rate for subject lines, click rate for content, conversion rate for CTAs.

## Deliverability

Reaching the inbox requires ongoing maintenance:

- **Authentication** -- Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records for your sending domain.
- **List hygiene** -- Remove hard bounces immediately. Suppress unengaged contacts after 90 days of inactivity.
- **Sending reputation** -- Warm up new domains and IPs gradually. Monitor blacklists.
- **Unsubscribe** -- Make it easy and one-click. A visible unsubscribe link reduces spam complaints.
- **Complaint rate** -- Keep spam complaints below 0.1%. Above 0.3% risks inbox placement across your entire domain.
